FAQ

Q. How accurate is this site?

A. This site does not claim to be accurate or precise in any way. It does not, for example, take into account any personal healh attributes into consideration. The information displayed is available from Life Tables provided by The Human Mortality Database and also performs extremely rough estimates based on those values.

Q. How do you come up with those estimates?

A. Given the values from the Life Table there's an assumption that there's roughly 365.25 days in a year (accounting for leap years) and it simply counts the days from the years given by the Life Table.

Q. On my birthday my numbers suddenly changed! What happened?

A. Statistically, probabilities of death fluctuate based on age according to the Life Table. For example, newborns are generally at a significantly higher risk than 1 year olds. Based on your new current age you get a different probability and therefore different statistics and estimates.

Q I want to look up ages that are even older than what I can pick here!

A. Most Life Tables do not have significant information past 110 years of age and therefore are generally grouped up as 110+. 110 years old is as old as you can pick.
